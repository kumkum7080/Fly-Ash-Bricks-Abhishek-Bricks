# CIVITAS’26: High-Early-Strength Fly Ash Brick Mix Design

## Industrial Problem Statement by Abhishek Bricks (IIT Indore)
Accelerating early strength development in Fly Ash–Lime–Gypsum (FAL-G) brick manufacturing to reduce the curing cycle from 15 days to **≤ 3 days (72 hours)** without capital expenditure.

---

## The Solution: Hybrid Chemical-Hydraulic Binder Matrix
Our team developed an optimized, high-performance mix design utilizing local industrial by-products (**GGBS**, **Rice Husk Ash**) alongside high-reactivity components (**Silica Fume**, **OPC 53**, and **Micro-synthetic fibers**). This system triggers ultra-fast early hydration kinetics, providing market-saleable compressive strength within 72 hours.

## Core Technical Domains Addressed

### 1. Accelerated Hydration Kinetics
* **OPC + GGBS Synergy:** Generates a quick exothermic reaction spike, chemically triggering latent hydraulic properties in the slag.
* **Particle Packing:** Silica Fume fills interstitial spaces between fly ash particles, acting as physical nucleation points for accelerated C-S-H gel growth.

### 2. Physical Structural Integrity ("Green Strength")
* Hybrid **Polypropylene & Micro-synthetic fibers** create a 3D internal mesh. This entirely eliminates structural edge-damage during immediate manual handling and multi-level pallet stacking.

### 3. Curing Optimization
* **0–24 Hours:** Ambient moisture-retention curing under tarpaulins to trap exothermic hydration heat.
* **24–72 Hours:** Rapid air-drying. Internal moisture is consumed cleanly by pozzolanic activity, eliminating the need for 15 days of manual water spraying (*Tari*).

## Industrial & Economic Benefits
* **4x Inventory Turnover:** Increases cycle rates from 2 cycles/month to 10+ cycles/month.
* **Minimized Raw Dependency:** Rice Husk Ash and GGBS inclusion decreases reliance on single-source thermal plant fly ash.
* **Freed Capital:** Reduces structural yard space bottlenecks by roughly 75%.
